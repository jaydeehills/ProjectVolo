01

What is a Prediction Market?

A prediction market is a place where people bet real money on whether something will happen — not sports betting exactly, but more like a stock market for future events.

Questions might be: "Will the Fed cut interest rates before December?" or "Will SpaceX land on Mars by 2027?" If you think YES, you buy a YES share. When the event resolves, whoever was right gets paid.

What does the price mean?

The price of a YES share is the market's collective estimate of the probability that the event happens.

YES Price
$0.45
≈ 45% chance of YES
YES Price
$0.90
≈ 90% chance of YES
YES Price
$0.08
≈ 8% chance of YES
The YES price and NO price always add up to approximately $1.00. If YES is $0.45, NO is about $0.55.

How do you make money?

Example Scenario
"Will the Chicago Bulls win the NBA Championship this season?"

Current YES price: $0.12 (market says 12% chance). You think they're actually a 30% chance. You buy 100 YES shares at $0.12 each → you spend $12.00.

✓ Bulls Win (YES resolves)
100 shares × $1.00 = $100.00 received
+$88 profit on a $12 bet
✗ Bulls Lose (NO resolves)
YES shares worth $0
−$12.00 lost

The key insight: you don't need to be right most of the time — you need to be right more often than the price implies. If the market says 12% and you correctly identify it should be 30%, you have profitable edge even though you'll lose that bet 70% of the time.

What is Polymarket?

Polymarket is the largest prediction market platform, running on the Polygon blockchain. It hosts hundreds of markets on politics, economics, sports, crypto, science, and more. All prices are in USDC — a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar. ProjectVolo reads data directly from Polymarket's public API.

What is ProjectVolo?

ProjectVolo is a research tool that automatically scans Polymarket for potential mispricings. Polymarket has hundreds of active markets — manually reading each one, forming a probability estimate, and comparing it to the current price would take hours every day.

1
Fetch all active, high-volume markets from Polymarket every 60 seconds
2
Run each question through Claude Haiku to estimate the true probability
3
Compare the AI estimate to the current market price to calculate the edge
4
Surface only the markets where the gap is large enough to be potentially interesting
ProjectVolo does not place bets. It does not manage money. It finds candidates for your own manual review.

05

What is Edge?

Edge is the core concept in prediction market investing. It's the difference between what you think the true probability is and what the market is currently pricing.

Edge = AI Estimate − Market Price

Why does edge matter?

In a perfectly efficient market, prices would always be correct. But markets are set by humans with limited time, incomplete information, and cognitive biases. When you find genuine edge — analytical insight the market hasn't fully priced in — you have a mathematical advantage. Think of it like poker: a professional doesn't win every hand, but by consistently getting money in with better odds, they profit over time.

Positive Edge → BUY YES
Market: 40% → AI: 65%
+25%
YES may be underpriced
Negative Edge → BUY NO
Market: 70% → AI: 45%
−25%
NO may be underpriced
ProjectVolo requires a minimum 8% edge before generating a Research Alert. Below that threshold, the signal is likely just noise from the AI's own imprecision or transaction costs.

What is Expected Value (EV)?

Expected Value is a mathematical concept that tells you how much you'd expect to win (or lose) on average if you made the same bet many times. In ProjectVolo, EV is calculated as:

EV = |Edge| × Confidence Weight
High Confidence
0.85×
Strongest weight
Medium Confidence
0.60×
Moderate weight
Low Confidence
0.15×
Auto-filtered to HOLD

A large edge with low confidence scores lower than a moderate edge with high confidence — which is the right behaviour. Research Alerts are sorted by EV from highest to lowest. Think of EV as a quality score, not a guarantee of profit.

Plain English example

Biased Coin Flip

You believe a coin lands heads 60% of the time. The market prices it at 50%. Your edge is 10%.

60% of the time
Win $1 → expected gain = $0.60
40% of the time
Lose $1 → expected loss = $0.40
Expected value per $1 bet: +$0.20. Over 100 bets you'd expect to make ~$20, even though you lose 40 individual bets.
07

What is Confidence?

Confidence tells you how certain the AI is in its probability estimate. It's the quality filter on the edge signal — a 30% edge from low confidence might be meaningless noise.

High
Solid base rate, good public data, unambiguous resolution criteria. E.g. Fed rate decisions, major elections, well-known sports.
Medium
Some relevant info but notable uncertainty. E.g. mid-tier political races, emerging market events, mixed evidence.
Low
Limited topic knowledge, highly unpredictable event, or unclear resolution criteria. Never generates BUY signals.
High confidence + large edge = worth serious attention.
Low confidence + large edge = probably noise.

The Full Pipeline

Here's exactly what happens, step by step, from market fetch to Research Alert appearing on screen.

1
Market Fetch every 60s
Backend calls Polymarket's Gamma API. Filters out markets with <$500k volume, closing within 7 days, or already closed.
2
Price Extraction
Extracts current YES and NO prices from Polymarket's outcomePrices field (JSON-encoded string).
3
Category Inference
Infers category (Politics, Sports, Crypto, etc.) from the event's ticker/slug using keyword matching — Polymarket doesn't provide a clean category field.
4
AI Estimation cached 24h
Calls Claude Haiku with the question, category, current price, and 8 calibration rules. Returns: estimated_probability, confidence, reasoning, key_factors. Result cached in memory + SQLite.
5
Edge Calculation
Computes edge, applies price-extremes filter (<3¢ or >97¢ → auto HOLD), applies confidence weight, determines signal, calculates EV.
6
Scan Cache 5 min TTL
Full scan result cached for 5 minutes. Concurrent requests get the previous cached result rather than triggering redundant scans.
7
Frontend Display polls every 30s
Dashboard polls /api/edge/scan, updates the Stats Bar, Research Alerts table, Edge Distribution Chart, and watchlisted market prices.

How to Place a Bet Using This Tool

ProjectVolo never touches your money. Here's the workflow for going from a Research Alert to an actual position on Polymarket.

1
Find a Research Alert — Focus on high confidence, edge >15%, and high EV (sorted to the top automatically).
2
Read the Research Report — Click the row to expand it. Does the AI's logic make sense? Is the reasoning specific or vague?
3
Do your own research — The AI has a knowledge cutoff. Google the topic, check for recent news, look at the Polymarket price history.
4
Visit Polymarket — Check the current price (it may have moved), volume, and order book liquidity.
5
Make your decision — You — not the AI — decide whether to trade. The AI is a starting point, not a directive.
6
Size your position — Never bet more than you can afford to lose. A reasonable starting point: 1–5% of total bankroll per trade.
7
Track your results — Record every trade: signal, reasoning, price paid, outcome. Over time this tells you if the signals actually work for you.

What the AI Actually Does

It's important to be honest about what Claude Haiku (the AI model powering ProjectVolo) actually is and isn't.

✓ What it knows
  • • Historical events, trends, statistics
  • • How political & economic systems work
  • • Sports team performance & player histories
  • • Scientific and technical topics
  • • Probabilistic reasoning
✗ What it doesn't know
  • • Anything after its training cutoff
  • • Real-time prices or live news
  • • Private or non-public information
  • • Obscure niche topics

How it reasons

1
Start with a base rate — what's the historical frequency of this type of event?
2
Update based on specific evidence it knows about this situation
3
Regress toward 50% to account for model overconfidence
4
Flag low knowledge — sets confidence to Low when information is limited
5
Respect mutually exclusive constraints — if 10 candidates compete, most should be well below 50%
Treat the AI like a well-read research analyst who's been off the grid for a few months. Smart and systematic, but potentially behind on recent news.

Limitations and Risks

This section is the most important in the guide. Please read it carefully.

⚠ Risk Factors
  • The AI can be wrong. A 65% estimate means you'll be wrong 35% of the time — and calibration isn't perfect.
  • Markets can be efficient. Sophisticated traders may have already priced in what the AI noticed.
  • No live data. A signal might be invalidated by news from yesterday. Always check current news.
  • Knowledge cutoff means stale reasoning on fast-moving situations.
  • Low-volume markets are riskier — even $500k+ volume markets can have limited liquidity.
  • Transaction costs eat into edge. An 8% signal might be marginal after Polymarket fees.
  • You can lose all of your money. Prediction market positions can go to zero.
This is not financial advice. ProjectVolo is a research tool built for educational exploration. Nothing in this guide or in the AI's output constitutes financial advice. All trading decisions are yours alone.

Glossary

Base Rate
Historical frequency of an event type. The AI always starts here before considering specific evidence.
BUY YES
Signal meaning YES may be underpriced. Edge > +8% required.
BUY NO
Signal meaning NO may be underpriced (YES overpriced). Edge < −8% required.
Calibration
How well a stated confidence matches reality. A 70% estimate should be right ~70% of the time.
Confidence
AI's self-reported certainty: Low, Medium, or High. Low never generates BUY signals.
Edge
AI Estimate − Market Price, in percentage points.
EV
Expected Value = |Edge| × Confidence Weight. Quality score for ranking Research Alerts.
Gamma API
Polymarket's public data API (gamma-api.polymarket.com) used by ProjectVolo.
HOLD
No action recommended — edge <8%, confidence too low, or price at an extreme.
Knowledge Cutoff
Date after which the AI has no information. Events after this date are unknown to the model.
Liquidity
How much money is being actively traded. Higher liquidity = easier to enter/exit positions.
Market Price
Current YES price on Polymarket, set by supply and demand. 0.45 = 45% implied probability.
MIN_VOLUME
Minimum total trading volume ($500,000) required for inclusion in analysis.
NO Share
Pays $1.00 if the event does NOT happen, $0 if it does.
Polymarket
The prediction market platform ProjectVolo reads from. Runs on Polygon blockchain, denominated in USDC.
Rate Limit
Anthropic API restriction on requests per minute. ProjectVolo handles with auto-retry + 3s delay.
Research Alert
Market where the AI found ≥8% gap between estimate and market price.
Scan Cache
5-minute server-side cache of full scan results. Prevents redundant re-processing.
SQLite
Lightweight database used to persist probability estimates across server restarts.
TTL
Time To Live. Estimate cache: 24h. Scan cache: 5min. Market fetch: 60s.
USDC
Stablecoin pegged to the US dollar. $1 USDC ≈ $1 USD. Used as Polymarket's currency.
YES Share
Pays $1.00 if the event happens, $0 if it doesn't.
